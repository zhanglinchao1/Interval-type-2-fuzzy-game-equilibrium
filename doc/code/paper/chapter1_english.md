# An Interval Type-2 Fuzzy Game Equilibrium Method for Collaborative Communication among Physical Agents

## Abstract

Collaborative communication among physical agents requires perception sharing, task coordination, and resource scheduling in dynamic environments, and its strategic payoffs are jointly affected by trusted interaction, communication timeliness, resource feasibility, and on-chain governance weights. Blockchain governance can enhance trusted multi-agent collaboration, but dynamic changes in on-chain reputation states and governance-rule weights reshape the off-chain strategic payoff structure, making deterministic payoff games insufficient for characterizing fuzzy boundaries and interval uncertainty. To address this issue, this paper proposes an interval type-2 fuzzy game equilibrium method for blockchain-governed collaborative communication among physical agents, and models and verifies it in an Internet of Vehicles collaborative communication scenario. The proposed method elevates the membership degrees of trusted interaction, communication timeliness, and resource feasibility to interval type-2 state inputs, and constructs interval type-2 fuzzy payoffs through payoff-layer weight projection. It then constructs defuzzified payoffs based on $\alpha$-cuts and defines a robust $\alpha$-Fuzzy Nash Equilibrium. Furthermore, the W-FBRI algorithm is designed to solve mixed-strategy equilibria, and a two-time-scale stability model is constructed for strategy replicator dynamics and on-chain governance-weight updates. MATLAB algorithm simulations and Veins-based Internet of Vehicles scenario simulations show that the proposed method can achieve stable equilibrium convergence, robust strategy selection, and interpretable governance regulation under state uncertainty and dynamic governance weights. This study provides a unified method for blockchain-governed collaborative communication among physical agents, covering fuzzy state modeling, interval payoff construction, equilibrium solving, and stability analysis.

**Keywords:** physical agents; interval type-2 fuzzy game; blockchain governance; Internet of Vehicles; evolutionary stability

# Chapter 1 Introduction

Physical agents are autonomous entities with capabilities for environmental perception, communication interaction, edge computing, strategic decision-making, and physical action. Vehicles, unmanned aerial vehicles, mobile robots, and industrial AGVs can all be regarded as typical forms of physical agents. With the development of collaborative perception, assisted driving, vehicle-road collaboration, and edge intelligence, individual autonomy is evolving toward multi-agent collaboration [1]. In Internet of Vehicles scenarios, vehicle-type physical agents need to conduct perception sharing, task coordination, and resource scheduling with infrastructure-type collaborative agents such as roadside units and edge servers. High vehicle mobility, frequent topology changes, and wireless-link fluctuations make collaborative payoffs jointly constrained by link quality, resource status, interaction trustworthiness, and governance rules.

Blockchain provides trusted recording, reputation maintenance, and rule execution mechanisms for collaborative communication among physical agents in the Internet of Vehicles, enabling vehicle behavior evidence, reputation states, and resource transaction constraints to be audited and reused in multi-agent environments. Existing studies have applied blockchain to trust management, reputation updating, privacy protection, and abnormal behavior identification in the Internet of Vehicles [2], [3]. However, when on-chain governance participates in collaborative communication decisions, reputation states and governance-rule weights continuously reshape the payoff structure of off-chain agents. Conventional deterministic payoff functions are therefore insufficient for characterizing fuzzy boundaries in state evaluation, variations in payoff intervals, and the influence of governance-weight updates on equilibrium stability.

In collaborative communication among physical agents, strategic payoffs are usually determined jointly by trusted interaction, communication timeliness, and resource feasibility. Trusted interaction is affected by historical behavior, on-chain reputation, neighbor feedback, and abnormal events. Communication timeliness is related to end-to-end delay, jitter, packet loss rate, and link congestion. Resource feasibility depends on computing load, bandwidth occupation, residual energy, instantaneous power consumption, and other states. Because these variables are accompanied by observation noise, prediction bias, and unclear semantic boundaries, interval type-2 fuzzy representation can explicitly describe uncertainty in the membership degree itself, and provides a unified expression for payoff upper and lower bounds, fuzzy dominance relations, and robust equilibrium judgment.

Game theory has been widely used in resource collaboration, task offloading, and strategy selection in the Internet of Vehicles and vehicular edge computing. Common methods include Nash games, matching games, potential games, and bargaining games [4], [5]. Fuzzy logic has also been used for uncertain state evaluation in IoV task offloading and resource scheduling, so as to handle decision ambiguity caused by high mobility, dynamic topology, and task urgency [6], [7]. In addition, existing research on resource aggregators maps CPU, bandwidth, energy, and power-consumption states into a resource feasibility membership degree $\mu_{\mathrm{res}}$, and proves its monotonicity, boundary correctness, bottleneck priority, and stability [8]. These studies provide foundations from the perspectives of trusted governance, game-theoretic scheduling, and fuzzy evaluation, and their technical details will be further reviewed in Chapter 2.

Although existing studies have laid a foundation for trusted governance, game-theoretic scheduling, and fuzzy state evaluation, three unresolved issues remain in blockchain-governed collaborative communication among physical agents. First, blockchain trust management mainly focuses on trusted records, reputation updates, and smart contract execution, while formal analysis is still insufficient on how on-chain governance weights enter strategic payoff functions and how they affect equilibrium stability. Second, existing game-theoretic resource scheduling methods are mostly based on deterministic payoffs, making it difficult to express payoff intervals and fuzzy dominance relations under the joint effect of trusted interaction, communication timeliness, and resource feasibility. Third, fuzzy evaluation methods can generate state membership degrees, but a closed theoretical chain has not yet been established from $\mu_{\mathrm{trust}}$, $\mu_{\mathrm{delay}}$, and $\mu_{\mathrm{res}}$ to interval type-2 fuzzy payoffs, fuzzy equilibrium solving, and on-chain governance stability.

To address these issues, this paper focuses on the following core problem: under dynamically adjusted on-chain governance rules, how can the three types of uncertain states, namely trusted interaction, communication timeliness, and resource feasibility, be transformed into a strategic payoff structure that is comparable, solvable, and suitable for stability analysis? Around this problem, this paper proposes an interval type-2 fuzzy game equilibrium method for blockchain-governed collaborative communication among physical agents, and uses collaborative communication in the Internet of Vehicles as a representative scenario for modeling and verification. The method incorporates fuzzy state modeling, interval payoff construction, equilibrium solving, and governance stability analysis into a unified framework.

The main contributions of this paper are as follows.

(1) An interval type-2 fuzzy payoff game model is proposed for blockchain-governed collaborative communication among physical agents. Trusted interaction, communication timeliness, and resource feasibility are incorporated into strategic payoff construction, and the regulatory effect of governance rules is characterized through a projection mechanism from on-chain governance weights to payoff-layer weights.

(2) A robust $\alpha$-Fuzzy Nash Equilibrium is defined, and its approximate equivalence relationship with the defuzzified game is proved. Based on $\alpha$-cuts in center-radius form, computable payoff intervals, equilibrium judgment, and an error budget are provided.

(3) A W-FBRI equilibrium-solving algorithm is designed for defuzzified payoffs. Mixed-strategy equilibria are solved through entropy-regularized soft best responses and damped updates, and global convergence to a unique fixed point under the contraction condition is proved.

(4) A two-time-scale stability analysis framework is constructed by coupling blockchain governance with replicator dynamics. The local evolutionary stability of the robust $\alpha$-FNE/Fuzzy-ESS is proved, and long-term stability under small-step governance updates is analyzed.

For experimental verification, this paper conducts MATLAB-based algorithm simulations and Veins-based Internet of Vehicles scenario simulations to verify the effectiveness of the proposed method in equilibrium convergence, robustness to state uncertainty, and stability of governance regulation.

The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Chapter 2 reviews related studies. Chapter 3 presents the system model, blockchain governance model, and state membership inputs. Chapter 4 constructs the interval type-2 fuzzy payoff game, defines $\alpha$-FNE, designs the W-FBRI algorithm, and analyzes the two-time-scale stability of blockchain governance and replicator dynamics. Chapter 5 conducts MATLAB and Veins simulation verification. Chapter 6 concludes the paper and discusses future research directions.
